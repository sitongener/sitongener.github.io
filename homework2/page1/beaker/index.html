The recommended daily amount of drinking water for humans varies.[1]
It depends on activity, age, health, and environment.
In the United States, the Adequate Intake for total water, based on median intakes,
is 4 litres (140 imp fl oz; 140 US fl oz) per day for males older than 18,
and 3 litres (110 imp fl oz; 100 US fl oz) per day for females over 18;
it assumes about 80% from drink and 20% from food.[2]
The European Food Safety Authority recommends 2.0 litres (70 imp fl oz; 68 US fl oz)
of total water per day for women and 2.5 litres (88 imp fl oz; 85 US fl oz) per day for men.[3]
